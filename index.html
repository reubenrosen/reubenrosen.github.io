Reuben Rosen

Electrical and Electronics Engineer, Biomedical Engineer, Software Developer

I completed my Bachelors in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Vellore Institute of Technology, ranked among the top 10 universities in the country for engineering. During my tenure I undertook a lot of undergraduate research, one of which has been accepted as a conference paper and is published in IEEE Xplore. (https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9691601)

I completed my Masters in Biomedical Engineering from Cornell University, ranked 12th in Biomedical Engineering in the country. I had the opportunity to work as a Design Engineer with Baxter International, as part of which I built a medical device- an AI based automated assistant for nurses to evaluate patients.

Currently I work as a GUI Developer at the Lampe Joint Department of Biomedical Engineering at NC State and UNC Chapel Hill. I built a website (Advanced Cell Annotation and Detection Tool) which has a 3 in one function: 1) allows the user to annotate cells in microscope images 2) Skip the hassle of annotating all the cells, and instead click on Detect SGN (Spiral Ganglion Neuron), using RetinaNet based deep learning technique for object detection. The user can also fine-tune the model for improved detections/adjusting the model to detect their own kind of cells. The source code is available on Github (https://github.com/reubenrosenNCSU/cellannotation/tree/main), and you can view the YouTube Demonstration here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IhLrQrVeXEQ&ab_channel=ReubenRosen

In 2022, I completed my internship at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO), Ministry of Defense, India. I worked under the Naval Physical and Oceanographic Laboratory (NPOL) where I built a security system to identify patients based on their voice. Essentially a voice recognition system. The project was later extended to be my capstone project, as an application which can detect COPD in patients based on the sound of their breathing.

I recently built SimpleMedic, which uses Natural Language Processing (NLP), specifically lemmetizing to tokenize input sentences into context, and compare it to a file of possible conditions. The BERT model was trained with a small CSV file containing sentences and their associated possible conditions. When you enter your symptoms, the program outputs the probability of different conditions. GitHub link: https://github.com/reubenrosen/SimpleMedic/tree/main
